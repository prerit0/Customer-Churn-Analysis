### 📖 Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the most critical problems faced by subscription-based businesses. Retaining existing customers is significantly more cost-effective than acquiring new ones.

This project analyzes customer churn behavior using exploratory data analysis (EDA) techniques to uncover patterns and actionable business insights that can help reduce churn.

The objective is to:

Identify key factors influencing customer churn

Discover high-risk customer segments

Provide business-driven retention strategies

### 📊 Dataset Information

Dataset: Telecom Customer Churn Dataset

Total Customers: ~7,000+

Features: 20+ variables including:

Demographics (Senior Citizen, Gender)

Services (Internet, Phone, Streaming, Security, Backup)

Account Information (Tenure, Contract Type, Payment Method)

Target Variable: Churn (Yes/No)

### 🔍 Key Insights from Analysis

1️⃣ Senior Citizens Show Higher Churn

Approximately 40% of senior citizen customers churned.

Indicates potential usability, pricing, or service experience issues.

2️⃣ Early Lifecycle Customers Are High Risk

Customers with tenure between 1–10 months churn the most.

Suggests weak onboarding or unmet early expectations.

3️⃣ Month-to-Month Contracts Have Highest Churn

Flexible plans allow customers to exit easily.

Long-term contracts significantly reduce churn.

4️⃣ Service Adoption Strongly Impacts Retention

Customers without the following services churn significantly more:

Online Security

Online Backup

Device Protection

Tech Support

Streaming TV

Insight: The more services a customer adopts, the lower the churn probability.

5️⃣ Fibre Optic Internet Users Show Higher Churn

Premium service customers may have higher expectations.

Indicates potential service quality or pricing dissatisfaction.

6️⃣ Manual Payment Customers Churn More

Customers not enrolled in automatic payment have higher churn.

Payment friction may reduce customer retention.

📈 Business Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following strategies are recommended:

### 🚀 Improve Early Customer Onboarding

Welcome campaigns

First 3-month engagement strategy

Early support assistance

### 💳 Promote Automatic Payments

Offer small discounts for auto-pay enrollment

Provide reward points or cashback

### 📦 Encourage Service Bundling

Bundle Internet + Security + Backup

Offer discounted combo plans

### 📆 Incentivize Long-Term Contracts

Discount for yearly subscriptions

Loyalty rewards

### 👵 Senior Citizen Retention Strategy

Dedicated support helpline

Simplified service plans

Personalized assistance
